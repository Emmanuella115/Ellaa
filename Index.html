A member of the Labour Party since 2022, Obi was the presidential candidate in the 2023 Nigerian presidential election. Born in Onitsha, Anambra State, he attended Christ the King College for his secondary education and the University of Nigeria, Nsukka for his tertiary education.

He began his career in banking and later went into politics when he contested for Anambra State Governor in 2003 under the All Progressives Grand Alliance. Although Chris Ngige was initially declared the winner, the result was annulled following a legal ruling. Obi was later declared the rightful winner and took office on 17 March 2006.

His administration was interrupted on 3 November 2006 when the state assembly removed him from office for alleged “gross misconduct.” The position devolved to his deputy, Dame Virginia Etiaba. Obi challenged the impeachment in court and was reinstated on 9 February 2007.

Later in May 2007, despite Obi’s ongoing tenure, elections were held and Andy Uba of the People’s Democratic Party (PDP) was declared governor. Obi again went to court, and the Supreme Court upheld his position, overturning Uba’s swearing-in. This allowed Obi to complete his term from 14 June 2007 to 17 March 2010. He was successfully re-elected and governed until 17 March 2014, when he handed over to his successor, Willie Obiano.

Goodluck Jonathan

Goodluck Ebele Azikiwe Jonathan (born 20 November 1957) is a Nigerian politician who served as the President of Nigeria from 2010 to 2015. Prior to that, he served as Vice President (2007–2010) under President Umaru Musa Yar’Adua and as Governor of Bayelsa State (2005–2007).

Jonathan assumed the presidency in 2010 after Yar’Adua’s death and was elected in his own right in 2011. His tenure focused on economic reforms and infrastructure development, but his administration also faced significant criticism over corruption and the Boko Haram insurgency. In 2015, he lost re-election to Muhammadu Buhari, marking the first peaceful transfer of power between two political parties in Nigerian history.
